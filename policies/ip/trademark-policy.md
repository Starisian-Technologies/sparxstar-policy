## Plain Language Summary

**What this policy is about**

This policy explains the brand names and logos we own, what you can and cannot do with them, and how to report someone misusing our brand or yours.

**Our trademarks**

We own the following brand names and logos:

- **SPARXSTAR** — the name and logo of our main platform
- **AI West Africa** and **AIWA** — the name and logo of our West African publishing and aggregation initiative
- **Starisian Technologies** — the name of our company

These names and logos are protected by trademark law. That means you need our permission to use them in most situations.

**What you CAN do with our brand names**

You may use our brand names without asking us when you are:

- Writing a news article, review, or commentary about the SPARXSTAR platform
- Referring to us in an academic or educational paper
- Clearly identifying us as the platform you are using in a factual way

This is called "nominative fair use." You are simply identifying us, not claiming affiliation.

**What you CANNOT do with our brand names**

You may NOT:

- Use our names or logos to suggest we sponsor, endorse, or are affiliated with you or your business, unless we have given you written permission
- Use our names or logos as part of your business name, product name, website name, or domain name
- Use our names or logos in hidden code, website metadata, or tags to direct people to your site
- Create logos or names that look confusingly similar to ours
- Mix our logos or brand elements with other brands without our written permission
- Pretend to be SPARXSTAR, AIWA, or anyone else affiliated with us

**You cannot upload content that infringes other people's trademarks either**

Just as we protect our own trademarks, you must respect other brands' trademarks when using the platform.

**How to request permission to use our brand**

If you are an authorized partner or have a legitimate reason to use our brand elements, contact us to request brand usage guidelines and permission.

**How to report trademark misuse**

If you see someone misusing our trademarks or if someone is using your trademark on our platform without permission, you can submit a trademark infringement complaint to us. We will review it and take appropriate action.

### Section 1 — Definitions

For purposes of this Trademark Policy, the following terms have the meanings set forth below:

1.1 **"Trademark"** means any word, name, symbol, device, or combination thereof that is used in commerce to identify and distinguish the goods or services of one party from those of others, and to indicate the source of the goods or services, and that is protected under 15 U.S.C. § 1051 et seq. (the Lanham Act), applicable state trademark law, and applicable international trademark law.

1.2 **"Service Mark"** means any word, name, symbol, device, or combination thereof used in commerce to identify and distinguish the services of one party from those of others, and to indicate the source of the services. As used in this Policy, "Trademark" includes Service Marks unless the context otherwise requires.

1.3 **"Trade Name"** means any name used by a person or entity to identify its business or vocation, including corporate names, fictitious business names (DBAs), and assumed names.

1.4 **"SPARXSTAR Marks"** means the collective trademarks, service marks, logos, trade dress, and brand identifiers associated with the SPARXSTAR platform, including without limitation the word mark SPARXSTAR, the SPARXSTAR logo and design marks, and associated taglines, domain-based identifiers, and brand elements as may be updated from time to time by Starisian Technologies.

1.5 **"AIWA Marks"** means the collective trademarks, service marks, logos, trade dress, and brand identifiers associated with the AI West Africa initiative, including without limitation the word marks AI WEST AFRICA and AIWA, associated logos and design marks, and domain-based identifiers, as may be updated from time to time by Starisian Technologies.

1.6 **"Starisian Technologies Marks"** means the word mark STARISIAN TECHNOLOGIES and associated logos, service marks, and brand identifiers used to identify Starisian Technologies as a legal entity and corporate brand.

1.7 **"Platform Marks"** means, collectively, the SPARXSTAR Marks, the AIWA Marks, and the Starisian Technologies Marks, and any other trademarks, service marks, logos, or brand identifiers owned or controlled by Starisian Technologies in connection with the Platform and its associated operations.

1.8 **"Authorized User"** means a User who has received express written authorization from Starisian Technologies to use the Platform Marks or specified elements thereof in connection with a specific, defined commercial or promotional purpose, pursuant to a brand usage agreement or authorized partner arrangement.

1.9 **"Nominative Fair Use"** means the non-trademark, non-commercial use of another party's trademark solely to refer to or identify that party's goods, services, or entity where: (a) the product or service in question is not readily identifiable without use of the trademark; (b) only so much of the mark is used as is reasonably necessary to identify the product or service; and (c) the use does not suggest sponsorship or endorsement by the trademark holder.

1.10 **"Confusingly Similar Mark"** means any mark, name, symbol, logo, or device that is sufficiently similar to a Platform Mark as to be likely to cause confusion, mistake, or deception among ordinary consumers as to the source, affiliation, sponsorship, or endorsement of goods or services.

1.11 **"User"** means any individual or entity who accesses, registers for, or uses the Platform.

1.12 **"Platform"** means the SPARXSTAR platform and associated services operated by Starisian Technologies, including all subdomains, APIs, applications, and integrated services.

1.13 **"Starisian Technologies"** means Starisian Technologies, the legal entity that owns and operates the Platform and the Platform Marks.

### Section 3 — Permitted Uses of Platform Marks

**3.1 Nominative Fair Use**

The following uses of the Platform Marks are permitted without express written authorization from Starisian Technologies, subject to the conditions set forth in Section 3.2:

  (a) **Editorial and Journalistic Reference** — Using the Platform Marks in news articles, blog posts, reviews, commentary, criticism, opinion pieces, and editorial content solely to identify and accurately refer to the SPARXSTAR platform, the AIWA initiative, or Starisian Technologies as the subject of discussion, provided such use does not imply sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation.

  (b) **Authorized Partner Promotion** — Referencing the Platform Marks in promotional materials by expressly authorized partners where such reference is made in accordance with the written brand usage guidelines issued by Starisian Technologies and is limited to accurately identifying the partner's relationship with the Platform.

  (c) **Academic and Educational Reference** — Using the Platform Marks in academic research, educational materials, course curricula, scholarly publications, and classroom instruction solely to identify and discuss the Platform, its services, or Starisian Technologies as a subject of study or analysis.

  (d) **Comparative Reference** — Using the Platform Marks in a factual comparative context to accurately identify the Platform in comparison to other products or services, provided such use does not suggest affiliation, endorsement, or sponsorship.

**3.2 Conditions for Permitted Use**

All permitted uses of the Platform Marks under Section 3.1 are subject to the following conditions:

  (a) Only the minimum use of the Platform Mark necessary to achieve the permitted purpose is made;

  (b) The use does not suggest that Starisian Technologies sponsors, endorses, approves, or is affiliated with the User or the User's content, products, or services;

  (c) The Platform Marks are not modified, distorted, or combined with other elements in a manner that degrades or alters their distinctive character;

  (d) The use does not dilute, tarnish, or otherwise harm the reputation or distinctive character of the Platform Marks;

  (e) The use is otherwise lawful under applicable trademark law.

**3.3 No License by Implication**

Nothing in this Trademark Policy creates, by implication, estoppel, or otherwise, any license or right in any Platform Mark beyond those expressly set forth in Section 3.1. Permission to use the Platform in accordance with the Platform's Terms of Service does not constitute authorization to use the Platform Marks in commerce.

### Section 4 — Prohibited Uses of Platform Marks

**4.1 Enumerated Prohibitions**

The following uses of the Platform Marks are expressly prohibited without the express prior written authorization of Starisian Technologies:

  (a) **Implied Affiliation or Endorsement** — Using the Platform Marks, or any Confusingly Similar Mark, in a manner that implies, suggests, or could reasonably be understood to imply affiliation, partnership, sponsorship, or endorsement by Starisian Technologies, SPARXSTAR, AIWA, or any associated entity, without express written authorization;

  (b) **Business and Product Names** — Using the Platform Marks or any Confusingly Similar Mark as or as part of a business name, trade name, company name, product name, service name, application name, or any commercial identifier;

  (c) **Domain Names and URLs** — Registering or using the Platform Marks or any Confusingly Similar Mark as or as part of a domain name, subdomain, URL path, username, social media handle, application identifier, or other online identifier;

  (d) **Metatags and Hidden Code** — Incorporating the Platform Marks or any Confusingly Similar Mark into website metatags, hidden HTML code, keyword advertising, search engine optimization (SEO) metadata, or any other technical mechanism designed or likely to redirect traffic or search results to a third-party website, application, or service;

  (e) **Confusingly Similar Marks** — Creating, adopting, registering, or using any Confusingly Similar Mark in connection with any product, service, or business activity in a manner likely to cause confusion as to the source, affiliation, or sponsorship of such product, service, or activity;

  (f) **Unauthorized Combination** — Using the Platform Marks in combination with other trademarks, brand elements, content, or identifiers without express written authorization, where such combination suggests co-branding, co-sponsorship, joint venture, or other commercial relationship not authorized by Starisian Technologies;

  (g) **Impersonation** — Impersonating SPARXSTAR, AIWA, Starisian Technologies, or any affiliated entity, including by creating accounts, profiles, personas, or communications designed to create the false impression that the User is acting as, for, or on behalf of Starisian Technologies or any affiliated entity;

  (h) **Modification and Distortion** — Modifying, altering, distorting, translating, combining, or creating derivative versions of the Platform Marks, including by altering colors, proportions, graphic elements, or typographic styling;

  (i) **Commercial Merchandise** — Placing the Platform Marks on physical or digital merchandise, promotional goods, apparel, or other items for commercial sale or distribution without express written authorization;

  (j) **NFTs and Digital Assets** — Minting, issuing, or distributing non-fungible tokens (NFTs) or other digital assets that incorporate the Platform Marks or that are designed to represent ownership of or rights in the Platform Marks without express written authorization.

**4.2 Consequences of Prohibited Use**

Violation of Section 4.1 may result in one or more of the following actions at Starisian Technologies' discretion: (a) a cease-and-desist demand; (b) removal of content or disabling of accounts; (c) reporting to domain registrars, social media platforms, or other third-party operators; (d) civil litigation for trademark infringement, unfair competition, dilution, false designation of origin, or other applicable claims under the Lanham Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1051 et seq., California Business and Professions Code § 17200 et seq., and applicable international trademark law; and (e) seeking injunctive relief, damages, disgorgement of profits, attorneys' fees, and other remedies available under applicable law.

### Section 6 — Trademark Infringement Complaint Procedure

**6.1 Submitting a Complaint**

Third-party trademark owners who believe that their trademark rights are being infringed by content or conduct on the Platform may submit a trademark infringement complaint to Starisian Technologies. Complaints may be submitted to the contact information designated for intellectual property complaints, as set forth on the Platform's legal page.

**6.2 Required Elements of a Trademark Complaint**

A valid trademark infringement complaint must include all of the following elements:

  (a) **Identification of the Trademark** — A description of the trademark that is allegedly being infringed, including the mark itself, the goods or services with which the mark is used, and, if applicable, the trademark registration number and jurisdiction of registration;

  (b) **Identification of Infringing Content** — Identification of the specific content on the Platform alleged to infringe the trademark, including the URL or other precise locator and a description of how the content infringes the mark;

  (c) **Complainant Contact Information** — The name, address, telephone number, and email address of the trademark owner or their authorized representative;

  (d) **Ownership Attestation** — A statement that the complainant is the owner of the trademark at issue, or is authorized to act on behalf of the trademark owner;

  (e) **Good Faith Statement** — A statement that the complainant has a good faith belief that the use of the trademark in the manner complained of is not authorized by the trademark owner, its agent, or applicable law;

  (f) **Accuracy Statement** — A statement that the information in the complaint is accurate and complete to the best of the complainant's knowledge; and

  (g) **Signature** — A physical or electronic signature of the trademark owner or their authorized representative.

**6.3 Platform Response Procedure**

Upon receipt of a complete and valid trademark infringement complaint, Starisian Technologies shall:

  (a) Review the complaint to assess whether it satisfies the requirements set forth in Section 6.2 and whether the alleged infringement is facially plausible;

  (b) Take appropriate action, which may include removal or modification of the allegedly infringing content, suspension or termination of the accused User's account, or other remedial measures as determined by Starisian Technologies in its reasonable discretion;

  (c) Notify the User whose content is at issue of the complaint and the action taken, where such notification is practicable and does not conflict with legal obligations;

  (d) Provide the complainant with notice of the action taken.

**6.4 User Response**

A User whose content has been removed or whose account has been restricted pursuant to a trademark complaint may submit a response to Starisian Technologies disputing the complaint. Starisian Technologies will review the response and take such additional action as it deems appropriate in its reasonable discretion. Starisian Technologies does not guarantee restoration of removed content or account reinstatement.

**6.5 False or Abusive Complaints**

Submitting a false, fraudulent, or materially misleading trademark infringement complaint may result in suspension or termination of the complainant's account, civil liability to the accused User, and other consequences under applicable law. Starisian Technologies reserves the right to reject complaints that appear to be submitted in bad faith or for purposes of harassment or competitive interference.
