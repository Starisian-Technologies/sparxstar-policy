## Plain Language Summary

*Layer 1 — Human Readable. Written at Grade 8 reading level.*

> What these terms mean for artists and rights holders distributing music through the platform.

**What we do:** We distribute your music to digital streaming platforms and stores worldwide on a non-exclusive basis, and we pass your royalties through to you minus our distribution fee.

**What you can do:** Upload your music, set your release information, choose your distribution territory, and earn royalties from streams and downloads.

**What is not allowed:** Uploading music you don't own the rights to, submitting fake streams or fraudulent engagement, or providing false metadata about your releases.

---

When you distribute music through SPARXSTAR, you keep full ownership of your music. We are not buying your recordings or your songs. You are giving us a license — a permission — to distribute your music on your behalf to streaming services and digital stores around the world.

You must own or control all the rights to everything you upload. That means the sound recording (the actual recorded track) AND the underlying musical composition (the melody and lyrics). If you used samples, you must have cleared them. If you recorded a cover song, you must have the mechanical license.

We earn money by keeping a percentage of revenue your music generates on streaming platforms. The rest goes to you. Specific percentages are set in your account agreement or service tier. We aim to be transparent about how royalties are calculated.

Fraud is taken seriously. If you artificially inflate your stream counts, use bots, or submit content you don't own, we will remove your music, close your account, and forfeit any accrued royalties.

Your lyrics are literary works with their own copyright. When you distribute through SPARXSTAR or AI West Africa services, we may analyze your lyrics for search, indexing, and classification purposes. We do not own your lyrics or the individual words in them. However, structured databases we build from analyzing content across the platform — things like semantic maps and indexed meaning systems — are platform property.

### 1. Definitions

**"Artist"** means any individual or entity who submits musical content to the platform for distribution.

**"Rights Holder"** means the person or entity that owns or controls the relevant rights in a Recording or Musical Work.

**"Recording"** or **"Sound Recording"** means the fixed audio rendition of a musical performance, as distinct from the underlying Musical Work.

**"Musical Work"** means the composition consisting of melody and lyrics, as distinct from any specific Recording thereof.

**"Distributor"** means Starisian Technologies, LLC, operating through the SPARXSTAR platform, acting as distribution agent on behalf of the Artist.

**"Digital Service Provider"** or **"DSP"** means any streaming service, digital download store, or online music retailer to which the Distributor delivers content, including but not limited to Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, Tidal, and Deezer.

**"ISRC"** means International Standard Recording Code, a unique identifier assigned to each Sound Recording.

**"UPC"** means Universal Product Code, a unique identifier assigned to each album, EP, or single release.

**"Net Revenue"** means gross revenue received by Distributor from DSPs for Artist's content, minus Distributor's distribution fee and any applicable withholding taxes or DSP charges.

**"Mechanical Rights"** means the right to reproduce a Musical Work in a Sound Recording.

**"Performance Rights"** means the right to publicly perform or communicate a Musical Work or Sound Recording.

**"Sync Rights"** means the right to synchronize a Musical Work or Sound Recording with visual media.

**"Micro-Sync"** means synchronization of a short clip of a Musical Work or Sound Recording with short-form video content, including content on social media platforms.

**"UGC Service"** means a user-generated content platform that allows users to upload content alongside music tracks (such as YouTube Content ID).

**"Lyrics"** means the text component of a Musical Work, constituting a literary work protected by copyright independently of the Recording.

**"Structured Dataset"** means a curated, organized compilation of data including semantic mappings, indexed metadata, and linguistic structures created by the platform from analysis of submitted content.

### 3. Eligibility and Rights Clearance

**3.1 Rights Ownership Requirement.** Artist represents and warrants that:

(a) Artist owns or controls all rights necessary to authorize the distribution of each submitted Recording, including rights in the Sound Recording and the underlying Musical Work;

(b) All samples, interpolations, or third-party elements incorporated in the Recording have been properly licensed or cleared;

(c) For cover songs, Artist has obtained a valid mechanical license from the rights holder or through an authorized mechanical licensing service;

(d) Artist has the full authority to enter into these Terms and grant the licenses herein.

**3.2 Rights Clearance Obligation.** Artist is solely responsible for clearing all rights before submission. The Distributor does not verify rights clearance and is not liable for infringement claims arising from Artist's failure to clear rights.

**3.3 Cultural Attribution.** Recordings that incorporate traditional, community-sourced, or culturally specific materials must include accurate attribution of origin at submission. Content subject to Traditional Knowledge labels must comply with the platform's TK Label Framework.

### 4. License Grant

**4.1 Non-Exclusive License.** Artist grants to Distributor a worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-bearing license to:

(a) Reproduce and store the Recording and associated metadata;

(b) Distribute and deliver the Recording to DSPs in the territories selected by Artist;

(c) License DSPs to make the Recording available for streaming, download, and other permitted digital uses;

(d) Collect and receive royalties and revenue from DSPs on Artist's behalf;

(e) Engage sub-distributors, sub-agents, and DSP partners as necessary to fulfill distribution.

**4.2 Non-Exclusive Nature.** This license is non-exclusive. Artist retains full ownership of the Recording and Musical Work and may distribute through other channels unless Artist has separately agreed to an exclusive arrangement.

**4.3 Sublicensing.** Distributor may sublicense the rights granted in Section 4.1 to DSPs and authorized sub-distributors solely for the purposes of digital distribution.

**4.4 Work-Made-For-Hire Exclusion.** These Terms do not constitute a work-made-for-hire agreement. Artist retains all copyright in submitted Recordings and Musical Works. No transfer of ownership occurs through submission or distribution.

**4.5 Survival.** The license granted herein survives termination to the extent necessary to fulfill existing DSP agreements, as set out in Section 14.

### 5. Metadata Requirements

**5.1 Required Metadata.** Artist must provide accurate and complete metadata for each release, including:

(a) Artist name (legal or professional name as it should appear on DSPs);

(b) Track title and album/EP/single title;

(c) Genre and sub-genre;

(d) Language of lyrics (if any);

(e) Explicit/clean content designation;

(f) ISRC code for each Recording;

(g) UPC code for each album or EP;

(h) Release date;

(i) Copyright and phonographic copyright year and owner;

(j) Composition rights holders (publisher, songwriter splits) where applicable.

**5.2 Metadata Accuracy Obligation.** Artist is solely responsible for the accuracy of all submitted metadata. False or misleading metadata — including fake artist names, fabricated composition credits, or misattributed origins — is grounds for immediate removal and account termination.

**5.3 Cultural Metadata.** Recordings derived from or incorporating traditional cultural materials must include accurate country of origin, ethnic or cultural attribution, and TK status designation where applicable.

### 6. Revenue and Royalties

**6.1 Revenue Collection.** Distributor will collect Net Revenue from DSPs on behalf of Artist.

**6.2 Revenue Share.** Distributor retains its distribution fee as set out in the applicable service tier or account agreement. The remainder of Net Revenue is remitted to Artist. Specific fee percentages are disclosed in the account onboarding documentation and may be updated with thirty (30) days' notice.

**6.3 Payment Schedule.** Distributor will remit Artist's share of Net Revenue on a monthly or quarterly basis (as specified in the service tier) following receipt and reconciliation of DSP payments. DSP payment timelines vary and may result in delays of up to ninety (90) days from the date of stream/download to the date of Distributor receipt.

**6.4 Payment Threshold.** Distributor may hold payment until accrued balance reaches a minimum payment threshold as specified in the service tier. Balances below threshold will roll forward to the next payment period.

**6.5 Payment Methods.** Distributor supports payment methods including bank transfer and other available methods disclosed in the account settings. Africa-specific payment methods including mobile money are planned and will be disclosed when available.

**6.6 Currency.** Payments are made in US dollars unless otherwise stated. Currency conversion losses are the Artist's responsibility.

**6.7 Withholding.** Distributor may withhold amounts as required by applicable tax law, including US withholding tax for non-US residents. Artist is responsible for providing applicable tax documentation.

**6.8 Royalty Disputes.** Artist must dispute any royalty statement within ninety (90) days of issuance. Undisputed statements are deemed accepted.

### 7. Anti-Fraud Provisions

**7.1 Prohibited Activity.** Artist must not:

(a) Artificially inflate stream counts, download counts, or engagement metrics using bots, paid services, or coordinated inauthentic activity;

(b) Manipulate playlist placement through deceptive means;

(c) Submit content owned by another party without authorization;

(d) Create duplicate accounts to circumvent enforcement;

(e) Provide false metadata to misrepresent the origin, ownership, or nature of a Recording.

**7.2 Consequences.** Violation of this Section will result in:

(a) Immediate removal of the affected content from distribution;

(b) Account suspension or permanent termination;

(c) Forfeiture of all accrued and unpaid royalties associated with the fraudulent activity;

(d) Potential referral to DSPs and relevant law enforcement.

### 8. User-Generated Content (UGC) Services

**8.1 Optional UGC Opt-In.** Artist may opt in to UGC distribution services, including YouTube Content ID and similar systems, which allow the platform to identify and monetize user-generated content that includes the Artist's Recording.

**8.2 Effect of Opt-In.** Upon opt-in, the platform is authorized to deliver the Recording to UGC detection systems and to claim and monetize matched content on Artist's behalf.

**8.3 Revenue Share.** UGC revenue is subject to the applicable revenue share as set out in the service tier.

### 9. Sync and Micro-Sync Licensing

**9.1 Optional Sync Opt-In.** Artist may opt in to the platform's sync licensing marketplace.

**9.2 Scope.** Upon opt-in, the platform may present the Recording for sync and micro-sync licensing opportunities, including placement in film, television, advertising, and short-form video content.

**9.3 Approval.** Sync placements may be subject to Artist approval depending on license tier. Micro-sync placements may be automated for efficiency where Artist has granted pre-approval.

**9.4 Revenue.** Sync licensing fees are shared per the applicable service tier agreement.

### 10. AI and Dataset Treatment of Lyrics and Literary Works

**10.1 Lyrics as Literary Works.** Lyrics are protected literary works under copyright law. Distributor does not acquire copyright in submitted Lyrics by virtue of distribution.

**10.2 Platform Analysis of Lyrics.** Platform may analyze Lyrics for purposes of:

(a) Search indexing and content discovery;

(b) Genre, mood, and thematic classification;

(c) Language identification and translation metadata;

(d) Cultural attribution and origin mapping.

**10.3 Structured Dataset Rights.** Structured databases, semantic mappings, and indexed linguistic representations created by the platform from analysis of lyrics and recordings across the platform are proprietary platform assets. Individual words extracted from Lyrics are not owned by the Platform.

**10.4 AI Training of Lyrics.** Lyrics are NOT included in AI model training by default. AI training use of Lyrics requires explicit opt-in by the Artist and is subject to a separate agreement.

**10.5 TK-Protected Lyrical Content.** Lyrics or vocal content designated as Traditional Knowledge (TK Red or TK Yellow restricted) are excluded from AI training and commercial exploitation without proper cultural authorization.

### 11. Content Moderation

**11.1 Compliance Requirement.** All submitted content must comply with the platform's Content Policy.

**11.2 Refusal Right.** Distributor may refuse to distribute content that:

(a) Contains explicit content not properly labeled;

(b) Violates applicable law in the distribution territory;

(c) Contains hate speech, incitement to violence, or content harmful to identified groups;

(d) Is subject to an unresolved copyright dispute.

**11.3 Labeling.** Explicit content must be designated as such at submission. Failure to label explicit content accurately is a violation of these Terms.

### 14. Term and Termination

**14.1 Term.** These Terms commence upon Artist's acceptance and continue until terminated by either party.

**14.2 Termination by Artist.** Artist may terminate distribution of any release by submitting a takedown request through the platform. Takedown from DSPs typically requires thirty (30) to ninety (90) days depending on DSP processing timelines.

**14.3 Termination by Distributor.** Distributor may terminate Artist's account immediately for breach of these Terms, including fraud, rights infringement, or violation of the Content Policy.

**14.4 Survival of License.** Upon termination, the license granted in Section 4 continues to the extent required by existing DSP agreements (including content already synchronized in user playlists), provided that Distributor takes reasonable steps to initiate takedown as soon as technically practicable.

**14.5 Final Payment.** Accrued royalties will be paid through the final applicable payment cycle after termination, subject to deduction of any amounts owed by Artist.
