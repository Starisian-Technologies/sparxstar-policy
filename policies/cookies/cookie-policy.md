> What this policy means for you.

**What is not allowed:** END USERS may not disable strictly necessary cookies required for PLATFORM security and authentication.

---

Cookies are small text files that get stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember things — like whether you are logged in, what language you prefer, or what is in your shopping cart. Most websites use cookies, and SPARXSTAR is no different.

We use four main types of cookies. Some are strictly necessary — without them the site simply cannot function. Others are functional cookies that remember your preferences. We also use analytics cookies that help us understand how people use the platform so we can improve it. Finally, some cookies support marketing features that show you relevant content or ads.

You have real control over many of these cookies. Your browser has built-in settings that let you block or delete cookies. We also provide a cookie preference tool on the platform where you can choose which non-essential cookies you accept. If you opt out of analytics or marketing cookies, some features may not work as well, but the core platform will still function.

The websites you visit that are powered by SPARXSTAR may set their own additional cookies. Those are the responsibility of the operator running that website, not ours. This policy explains what we set as the platform provider. If a site you visit has its own cookie policy, that policy will tell you about any additional cookies that site sets.

### 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Cookie Policy have the meanings given to them in the Master Terms of Service and the Privacy Policy. The following additional terms apply:

**1.1 "Cookie"** means a small text file placed on a User's device by a website server that stores information about the User's visit and device.

**1.2 "Strictly Necessary Cookies"** means Cookies that are essential to the basic functioning of the Platform or Services, including authentication, session management, security, and fraud prevention. These Cookies cannot be disabled without causing the Platform or Services to malfunction or become insecure.

**1.3 "Functional Cookies"** means Cookies that enable enhanced functionality and personalization of the Platform, such as remembering language preferences, display preferences, and other User settings. These Cookies are not essential to core Platform function but meaningfully improve User experience.

**1.4 "Analytics and Performance Cookies"** means Cookies used to collect information about how Users interact with the Platform, including pages visited, features used, time spent, error events, and traffic sources, for the purpose of measuring and improving Platform performance and User experience.

**1.5 "Marketing and Targeting Cookies"** means Cookies used to build profiles about Users' interests and online behavior for the purpose of displaying relevant advertisements or content, to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns, and to limit the frequency with which advertisements are shown.

**1.6 "First-Party Cookies"** means Cookies set directly by the Company's Platform Domains.

**1.7 "Third-Party Cookies"** means Cookies set by third-party domains, including analytics providers, advertising networks, social media platforms, and other service providers, through their integration with the Platform.

**1.8 "Similar Technologies"** means technologies that function similarly to Cookies, including pixel tags, web beacons, local storage objects (such as HTML5 localStorage and sessionStorage), device fingerprinting, and SDKs.

**1.9 "Cookie Consent"** means a User's express agreement, given through a consent mechanism, to the placement of non-Strictly-Necessary Cookies on their device, to the extent required by Applicable Law.

**1.10 "Operator Cookies"** means Cookies and Similar Technologies set by a Client Operator on their own domain or through their own integrations with the Platform, separately from Company-set Cookies.

### 3. Categories of Cookies the Platform Uses

**3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies.**

  (a) *Purpose.* Strictly Necessary Cookies are required for the Platform to function and cannot be disabled. They enable core capabilities including User authentication, session management, security token validation, CSRF protection, load balancing, access control, and fraud prevention.

  (b) *Examples of use.* Keeping You logged in during a session; validating that form submissions originate from legitimate Users; routing Your requests to the appropriate platform servers; and maintaining the security of Your session.

  (c) *Consent requirement.* Strictly Necessary Cookies do not require Your consent because they are necessary for the provision of the Platform. However, the Company provides this disclosure in the interest of transparency.

  (d) *Retention.* Strictly Necessary Cookies typically expire at the end of a browser session or after a defined short period required for security purposes.

**3.2 Functional Cookies.**

  (a) *Purpose.* Functional Cookies enable the Platform to remember choices You make and to provide enhanced features tailored to Your preferences.

  (b) *Examples of use.* Remembering Your language setting; storing display preferences such as dark mode or font size; remembering Your preferred content region or currency; and maintaining the state of interactive features.

  (c) *Consent requirement.* Where required by Applicable Law, Functional Cookies are placed only with Your Cookie Consent. Where Applicable Law does not require consent, the Company places Functional Cookies on the basis of its legitimate interest in providing a functional and personalized User experience.

  (d) *Retention.* Functional Cookies are typically persistent cookies with defined expiration periods ranging from thirty (30) days to two (2) years, depending on the specific function.

**3.3 Analytics and Performance Cookies.**

  (a) *Purpose.* Analytics and Performance Cookies collect information about how Users interact with the Platform. This information is used in aggregate to measure and improve Platform performance, identify and fix errors, understand content engagement, and make informed product decisions.

  (b) *Examples of use.* Recording which pages are visited and in what order; measuring how long Users spend on particular features; identifying which features generate errors; tracking referral sources; and measuring page load speeds.

  (c) *First-party and third-party analytics.* The Company uses both first-party analytics tools and third-party analytics services. Third-party analytics providers receive analytics data subject to their own privacy and data use policies. The Company takes reasonable steps to ensure that analytics data is used only for analytics purposes and is not combined with personally identifiable information by third-party analytics providers without appropriate safeguards.

  (d) *Consent requirement.* Where required by Applicable Law, Analytics and Performance Cookies are placed only with Your Cookie Consent.

  (e) *Retention.* Analytics Cookies may be session-based or persistent. Persistent analytics Cookies typically expire within thirteen (13) months.

**3.4 Marketing and Targeting Cookies.**

  (a) *Purpose.* Marketing and Targeting Cookies track Your online activity across the Platform and, where applicable, across third-party sites to build interest profiles, display relevant advertisements and content, measure advertising campaign effectiveness, and limit advertisement frequency.

  (b) *Examples of use.* Displaying advertisements relevant to content You have viewed; limiting how many times You see a particular advertisement; measuring whether You took action after viewing an advertisement; and enabling retargeting campaigns by advertising partners.

  (c) *Third-party advertising.* Marketing and Targeting Cookies are predominantly set by third-party advertising and marketing technology partners integrated with the Platform. Those partners operate their own advertising networks and may combine data collected on the Platform with data collected on other sites. Their data use is governed by their own privacy and cookie policies.

  (d) *Consent requirement.* Marketing and Targeting Cookies require Your express Cookie Consent where required by Applicable Law. You may opt out of Marketing and Targeting Cookies as described in Section 6.

  (e) *Retention.* Marketing and Targeting Cookies may be session-based or persistent. Persistent Marketing Cookies typically expire within ninety (90) days to two (2) years, depending on the specific partner and purpose.
