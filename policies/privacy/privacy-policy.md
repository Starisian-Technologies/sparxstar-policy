## Plain Language Summary

*Layer 1 — Human Readable. Written at Grade 8 reading level. Uses "you" and "we". Short sentences. No passive voice where avoidable.*

> What this policy means for you.

**What we do:** We collect information about how you use the SPARXSTAR platform so we can operate our services, keep them secure, and improve them.

**What you can do:** You can access, correct, delete, or download your personal data, and you can opt out of marketing communications at any time.

**What is not allowed:** We do not sell your personal data to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

---

When you use SPARXSTAR — or any website powered by our platform — we collect some information about you. This helps us run the platform, protect it from abuse, and make it better for everyone.

We collect information in three main ways. First, you give us information directly when you sign up, fill out forms, make purchases, or contact support. Second, we collect information automatically when you use the platform, such as your device type, browser, IP address, and which pages you visit. Third, other companies we work with — like payment processors and analytics providers — may share information with us as part of delivering our services.

We use your information to deliver services, process transactions, keep the platform safe, personalize your experience, and send you relevant communications. We may also use your content and data to train and improve our AI systems. If you do not want us to send you marketing messages, you can opt out at any time using the unsubscribe link in any email or by updating your account preferences.

We share your information with companies that help us operate the platform — such as cloud hosting providers, payment processors, shipping companies, and analytics tools. We do not sell your personal data to third parties for their own marketing. Some websites powered by our platform are run by separate businesses called "operators." Those operators may collect their own data about you. This policy covers what we collect as the platform provider.

If you are in California, you have specific rights under California privacy law. If you are in the European Union or United Kingdom, an EU/UK privacy overlay applies. We take children's privacy seriously and do not collect data from children under 13 without a parent's permission.

### 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Privacy Policy have the meanings given to them in the Master Terms of Service. The following additional terms apply:

**1.1 "Personal Data"** means any information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, to a natural person, as defined or recognized under Applicable Law, including but not limited to the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") and the General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR").

**1.2 "Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**1.3 "Data Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data, as defined under applicable data protection law.

**1.4 "Data Processor"** means an entity that Processes Personal Data on behalf of a Data Controller, as defined under applicable data protection law.

**1.5 "Service Provider"** means a company or individual that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Company, subject to written data processing obligations consistent with applicable law.

**1.6 "Platform Data"** means data collected by the Company in connection with the operation, security, analytics, and improvement of the Platform, including data collected across all domains powered by Platform infrastructure.

**1.7 "Operator Data"** means data collected by a Client Operator through their own site or application for their own purposes and under their own privacy disclosures.

**1.8 "Sensitive Personal Data"** means categories of Personal Data that receive heightened protection under Applicable Law, including but not limited to government-issued identifiers, financial account data, health or medical data, biometric data, precise geolocation, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, and data about minors.

**13.3 De-Identification.** Where the Company processes Personal Data for analytics, research, or AI training purposes, it uses de-identification, pseudonymization, or aggregation techniques to reduce identifiability where practicable, consistent with the purposes for which the data is processed.

**13.4 Sensitive Data Caution.** The Company applies additional safeguards to Sensitive Personal Data, including additional access controls, enhanced encryption, and heightened data minimization practices.
