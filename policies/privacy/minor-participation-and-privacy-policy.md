# Minor Participation and Privacy Policy

## Plain Language Summary

> What this policy means for you.

**What we do:** We protect participants under the age of digital consent through guardian-based consent, strict identity separation, and non-overrideable privacy safeguards.

**What you can do:** Parents and guardians can confirm consent status, revoke consent at any time, and request export or deletion of a minor's data under applicable law.

**What is not allowed:** No minor may participate without documented guardian consent, and no minor data may be used for AI training without explicit guardian consent and governance review.

---

This policy applies when a participant under the age of digital consent in their jurisdiction uses a product built on the platform. A minor cannot agree to terms independently. Consent must be provided by a parent, legal guardian, or authorized institution.

Consent may be provided through a platform e-signature process or by signed paper agreement returned through the administering institution. Consent is recorded only after the signed agreement is processed by platform systems.

When consent is processed, platform systems generate a unique internal code that links the signed agreement to the minor account while preserving identity separation in product systems. The child's real name remains on signed agreement records and is not stored alongside product activity data.

Minor protections apply automatically and cannot be overridden by administrators, teachers, or client organizations. These protections include no permanent storage of a child's real voice, strict privacy defaults, prohibition on personal ranking use, and prohibition on AI model training use without explicit guardian consent and governance controls.

Parents and guardians may review consent status, revoke consent at any time, and request data export or deletion subject to applicable law. When consent is revoked, protections escalate, consent-dependent retained data is quarantined or destroyed under retention rules, and limited anonymous use may continue where institution rules permit.

When a participant reaches the local age of digital consent, consent authority transitions from guardian to participant without loss of account continuity or contribution history.

## Full Legal Text

### 1. Scope and Covered Participants

**1.1 Covered Participants.** This Policy applies to any participant registered as under the age of digital consent in the participant's applicable jurisdiction ("Minor Participant") who uses a Platform product or service.

**1.2 Covered Parties.** This Policy applies to the Company, Platform Operators, administering institutions, and any authorized personnel who process Minor Participant enrollment, participation, support, or recovery.

---

### 2. Consent Requirements

**2.1 Guardian Consent Requirement.** A Minor Participant may not independently accept terms or provide legally sufficient consent for participation. Participation requires consent by a parent, legal guardian, or authorized institution acting within lawful authority.

**2.2 Permitted Consent Channels.** Consent may be captured through:

(a) digital signature flow provided by the Platform; or

(b) physical signed agreement collected by the administering institution and submitted for Platform processing.

**2.3 Consent Recording Rule.** Consent is effective only after a signed agreement is processed and recorded by Platform systems. No Minor Participant may be activated for normal participation without documented consent on file.

---

### 3. Identity Separation and Internal Coding

**3.1 Internal Enrollment Code.** Upon consent processing, the Platform generates a unique internal code linking consent records to the Minor Participant account.

**3.2 Segregation Principle.** The Minor Participant's legal name and signed agreement identity records must be segregated from contribution, activity, and learning data in product systems.

**3.3 Handling Limitation.** Internal enrollment codes are administrative artifacts and are not required to be presented to, memorized by, or managed directly by Minor Participants.

---

### 4. Mandatory Minor Safeguards

The following safeguards are mandatory for all Minor Participants and may not be disabled by institution administrators, teachers, client organizations, or other local operators:

**4.1 No Permanent Voice Storage.** A Minor Participant's real voice may not be stored as a permanent audio asset. Audio captured during permitted use must be processed and destroyed under applicable retention controls, with only non-identifying learning outputs retained where permitted.

**4.2 Strict Privacy Default.** Minor-generated data must be classified and processed under the highest privacy controls available by default, unless a narrower use is expressly authorized by valid guardian consent and applicable law.

**4.3 No Personal Ranking or Penalization Use.** Minor learning data may not be used to publicly rank, shame, discipline, or penalize a Minor Participant and may not be disclosed as comparative performance scoring to other participants.

**4.4 AI Training Restriction.** Minor contributions may not be used for AI model training unless explicit guardian consent authorizes that specific use and the contribution has passed required human review and governance controls.

---

### 5. Parent and Guardian Rights

**5.1 Consent Status Review.** Parents and guardians may request confirmation of consent status through the administering institution or Company support channels.

**5.2 Revocation.** Guardian consent may be revoked at any time. Revocation takes effect immediately upon validated receipt.

**5.3 Revocation Effects.** Upon revocation:

(a) account protections for the Minor Participant shall escalate to the highest available level;

(b) retained data requiring consent shall be quarantined or destroyed under applicable retention and legal-obligation rules; and

(c) participation may continue only in limited anonymous mode where permitted by the administering institution and applicable law.

**5.4 Access, Export, and Deletion Requests.** Parents and guardians may request access, export, correction, or deletion of Minor Participant data, subject to applicable law and lawful retention obligations.

---

### 6. Age-of-Consent Transition

When a Minor Participant reaches the age of digital consent in the applicable jurisdiction, consent authority transitions from guardian to participant. Account continuity, contribution history, and associated records remain intact unless deletion is lawfully required.

---

### 7. Account Recovery

Recovery for Minor Participant accounts shall be administered by institution administrators or Company support using identity verification procedures tied to signed agreement records. Minor Participants are not required to complete technical recovery procedures directly.

---

### 8. Institutional Responsibilities

Administering institutions are responsible for:

(a) distributing and collecting guardian consent agreements;

(b) delivering signed agreements, digital or physical, for Platform processing;

(c) securing physical consent records under appropriate safeguards;

(d) communicating consent revocation requests for immediate processing; and

(e) restricting internal enrollment code use to authorized recovery and administrative functions.

---

### 9. Contact

Questions regarding this Policy, rights under this Policy, or Minor Participant data handling may be directed to Company support or the administering institution.
