## Plain Language Summary

*Layer 1 — Human Readable. Written for artists, contributors, and users — not lawyers.*

> How we expect people to treat each other on our platform, and what happens when they don't.

**What we do:** We maintain a platform where artists, creators, and community members can collaborate — and we enforce rules that keep it safe and respectful for everyone.

**What you can do:** Participate, create, collaborate, disagree, and express yourself — as long as you treat others with basic respect.

**What is not allowed:** Harassment, threats, impersonation, spam, fraud, hate speech, or any conduct that harms or intimidates other users.

---

This policy governs how people behave on our platform. It applies to everyone who uses SPARXSTAR or AIWA — artists, listeners, developers, and any other user — across all platform surfaces including messaging, comments, community spaces, and profile pages.

We built SPARXSTAR to support artists and cultural creators. That means the community matters. We expect every person on the platform to treat others the way they would want to be treated — with honesty, respect, and good faith.

When someone violates these standards, we take action. Depending on how serious the violation is, that could mean a warning, a temporary suspension, or a permanent ban. For serious violations that break the law, we may involve law enforcement.

We do not tolerate conduct that makes others feel unsafe. Harassment is never acceptable. Threats — even ones framed as jokes — are never acceptable. Impersonating another person or brand is never acceptable.

### 1. Definitions

**"Company"** means Starisian Technologies, LLC, and its affiliates, subsidiaries, and authorized operators, collectively operating the SPARXSTAR and AIWA platforms.

**"Platform"** means all websites, applications, APIs, and services operated by the Company, including but not limited to SPARXSTAR, AIWA, and associated Starisian Technologies properties.

**"User"** means any individual or entity that accesses, registers with, or uses the Platform in any capacity.

**"Community Conduct"** means any act, communication, submission, or behavior carried out by a User on or through the Platform that affects other Users or the Platform community.

**"Prohibited Conduct"** means any conduct defined in Section 3 of this Policy.

---

### 2. Scope and Applicability

This Policy governs behavioral conduct by Users on the Platform. It applies globally to all Users, subject to the jurisdiction overlays identified in Section 8 below. This Policy governs human behavior only; rules governing what content may be submitted or published on the Platform are addressed separately in the Content Policy.

This Policy is incorporated by reference into the SPARXSTAR Master Terms of Service. Acceptance of the Master Terms of Service constitutes acceptance of this Policy.

---

### 3. Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited on the Platform:

**(a) Harassment and Intimidation.** Any conduct designed to intimidate, threaten, humiliate, or cause emotional distress to another User, including but not limited to repeated unwanted contact, targeted public attacks, and coordinated mass reporting intended to harm a User's account.

**(b) Threats of Violence.** Any communication that threatens physical harm to a person or group, whether directed at a specific individual or a class of people, whether communicated publicly or privately.

**(c) Hate Speech.** Any content or conduct that dehumanizes, attacks, or promotes discrimination against individuals or groups based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.

**(d) Impersonation.** Representing oneself as another person, artist, organization, or brand — including through usernames, profile images, or communications — in a manner that deceives or misleads others.

**(e) Spam and Fraudulent Activity.** Sending unsolicited bulk messages, creating fake accounts, engaging in coordinated inauthentic behavior, operating bots, or any activity intended to deceive the Platform's systems or other Users.

**(f) Unauthorized Data Collection.** Collecting personal information from other Users without their explicit consent or in violation of applicable law.

**(g) Interference with Platform Operations.** Any action that disrupts, overloads, or interferes with the technical operation of the Platform, including denial-of-service attacks, scraping in violation of the Company's terms, or circumventing security measures.

---

### 4. User Reporting

Users who experience or witness Prohibited Conduct may report it through the Platform's designated reporting mechanism. The Company will review reports and may take action as described in Section 5.

The Company does not guarantee that all reported conduct will result in enforcement action. The Company reserves the right to determine, in its sole discretion, whether a violation has occurred.

---

### 5. Enforcement and Remedies

Upon determining that a violation of this Policy has occurred, the Company may, in its sole discretion, take one or more of the following actions:

**(a)** Issue a warning to the User;

**(b)** Temporarily restrict the User's access to specific Platform features;

**(c)** Suspend the User's account for a defined period;

**(d)** Permanently terminate the User's access to the Platform;

**(e)** Remove or restrict visibility of content associated with the violation;

**(f)** Preserve records of the violation and, where required by law or where the Company determines it is in the public interest, report the conduct to relevant law enforcement authorities.

The Company may act without prior notice where delay would risk harm to another User, the Platform, or third parties.

---

### 6. Appeals

A User whose account has been suspended or terminated under this Policy may submit an appeal to the Company within thirty (30) days of the enforcement action. Appeals must be submitted through the Platform's designated appeals process. The Company will review appeals in good faith but retains final decision-making authority.

---

### 7. Cultural Conduct Standards

The Platform serves Indigenous, diasporic, and culturally distinct communities whose values around respect, elder authority, and sacred knowledge may exceed the baseline standards set in this Policy. Users engaging with cultural content, communities, or Traditional Knowledge materials must also comply with the applicable Cultural Protocol Overlays, including the TK Label Framework.
