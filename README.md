## Drafting Methodology

1. All policy language is modeled on established enterprise-grade terms from reference sources. See [`docs/source-model-analysis/reference-map.md`](docs/source-model-analysis/reference-map.md).
2. No verbatim copying. Structural modeling only.
3. California law governs by default. Venue: Los Angeles County, California.
4. Gambia and African regional operational overlays are supported.
5. Cultural governance protocols are reserved for TK / sacred content restrictions.
6. All policies use layered architecture: Master Terms → Policy Modules → Jurisdiction Overlays → Cultural Overlays.

---

## Source Modeling Philosophy

Policy clauses are modeled structurally from recognized industry-leading legal frameworks. These models inform clause structure, scope of rights, and limitation frameworks only. No text is reproduced verbatim. See [`docs/source-model-analysis/reference-map.md`](docs/source-model-analysis/reference-map.md) and [`docs/research/source-authority-index.md`](docs/research/source-authority-index.md).

## Versioning Rules

- Every policy file carries a metadata header: `version`, `date`, `status`, `jurisdiction`, `policyType`, `dependsOnMasterTerms`, `supersedes`.
- All policies are independently versioned.
- Version history is maintained in [`docs/version-history/`](docs/version-history/).
- Status progression: `planned → drafting → review → approved → active → archived`.

## Core Legal Principles

1. California law governs by default.
2. Venue jurisdiction: Los Angeles County, California.
3. Global use permitted.
4. Gambia and African operation supported.
5. Platform rights are broad but not culturally extractive.
6. Users retain ownership unless explicitly assigned.
7. Platform receives broad sublicensable operational license.
8. No work-made-for-hire (WMFH) for normal users.
9. WMFH permitted only for employees, commissioned contractors, and expressly contracted productions.
